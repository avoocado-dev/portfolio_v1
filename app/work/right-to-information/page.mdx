## The problem

Article 7 of the EU Pay Transparency Directive gives every employee in the EU the right to request — and receive within two months — a comparison of their own pay against the average pay of men and women doing work of equal value in their organization. For employers, that's a regulatory obligation with real teeth: late responses, incomplete responses, or inconsistent responses can each trigger penalties.

The catch is that there's no single right way to deliver the information. Some employers want to push the comparison directly into their HRIS so it shows up in the employee's existing self-service portal. Others want a one-click report they can email. Others want an API they consume into their own internal tools. Right to Information had to support all of them.

### The public API

{/* TODO: describe your role on the API. Did you spec the contract? Build
    the consumption-side client? Or focus on the admin UI that triggers it? */}

- **`POST /v1/workspace/{id}/rightToInfo/`** — returns per-employee pay-comparison data as JSON (own pay annual + hourly, average pay for men in the SSG, average pay for women in the SSG). Customers consume this into their own systems.
- **`POST /v1/workspace/{id}/rightToInfo/hris`** — same data, but pushed via Workato into the customer's HRIS (Workday and others).
